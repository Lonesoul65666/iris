## BUDGET-MATH
SUMMARY: The three-lane model itself (budgetLanes.ts) is internally sound — laneOf/isOverBudget/FIXED_OVER_TOLERANCE do what their comments claim, and the insights engine and calculateBudgetSummary apply it correctly. The two systemic problems are: (1) every 'partial month' guard is transaction-count based (>10/>=10/<=10 in different files), which fails at this household's ~180 txns/month — the in-progress month counts as 'full' within days, polluting the savings scorecard's banked/under-base/trend numbers, deflating all bucket averages mid-month, and becoming the Budget overview's default month; and (2) the reserve/work exclusion sets are applied inconsistently across surfaces, so 'Saved This Month', the Cash Flow sub-score, the savings scorecard month strip, and the Dashboard surplus can all disagree about the same month, with the scorecard still painting the April tax payment red. Beyond those, there are several contained but real bugs: the dashboard CashFlowBar double-subtracts investing, refunds are booked as income with the expense-offset branch unreachable, work travel auto-categorized by Teller is invisible to the work-expense trackers, the avg-mode drilldown compares multi-month totals to monthly budgets and annualizes them x12, and UTC date parsing drops 1st-of-month paychecks from month windows.

## PRODUCT-GAPS
SUMMARY: Against the Monarch/YNAB/Copilot table-stakes bar, Iris's budget side has genuinely strong bones — transaction search/filtering is near parity, recurring detection is well-engineered, refund/transfer/reimbursement typing exists, a full JSON backup works, and goal math plus an audit-log backend are already written. The systemic weakness is time: there is no real current-month axis (every "this month" surface shows multi-month averages, making pace alerts and the dashboard pulse incoherent), no month-close or budget versioning, and no forward look (forecast). The second theme is unfinished promises: the Settings panel advertises critical alerts with zero implementation and no delivery channel outside the app, the Reserve lane's "it's funded monthly" premise is never actually tracked against a balance, and a complete GoalTracker UI ships in the bundle but renders nowhere. Sharpest quick wins: net refunds per-category (small bug, distorts the exact flexible lanes the new model highlights) and fix the 180-day lookback that makes quarterly/yearly bill detection mathematically impossible.

## DATA-PIPELINE
SUMMARY: The bank-data pipeline's happy path is genuinely solid — single-transaction upserts with rollback, deterministic teller_<id> idempotency, batch tags for reversibility, and a thoughtful edit-preserving jsonb merge. The robustness problems cluster at the edges where errors and time pass: Teller failures inside a sync (429s, per-account errors) are collected server-side but never surface to the UI, so partial syncs are recorded as fresh successes and the client's entire rate-limit branch is dead code; the 14-day delta window is anchored to 'now' rather than the last sync, so any lapse over two weeks silently and permanently drops transactions; and pending-transaction lifecycle is entirely unhandled (status never read or persisted, nothing ever deleted), so voided holds become permanent phantom spend. A second tier of drift bugs — re-enrollment leaving stale active connectors, the user's 135 merchant mappings never applying to Teller imports, deleted rows resurrecting inside the sync window, and the hardcoded /ABNORMAL/ employer regex with Scott's job change imminent — will each produce quietly wrong budget numbers within months if not addressed.
